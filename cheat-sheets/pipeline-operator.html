H2S — Hydrogen Sulfide

Concentration (ppm)Effect
0.01 – 0.3Detectable rotten egg smell
10OEL (Occupational Exposure Limit) — 8 hr
15STEL — 15 minute short-term limit
100IDLH — Immediately Dangerous to Life or Health
500+Loss of consciousness within minutes
1000+Immediate collapse, death
Smell is NOT a warning sign. H2S paralyzes your sense of smell at higher concentrations. Always wear a monitor. H2S Alive certification required for sour gas work in Western Canada.

Trench Setup Rules

  • Spoil pile minimum 0.6 m (2 ft) from trench edge
  • Ladder access every 7.5 m (25 ft) of trench
  • Worker entry only after daily inspection by competent person
  • Shoring rated for the soil type and depth
  • Standing water = stop work, pump out, reassess

Side Boom Basics

  • Three-point lift: minimum 3 side booms for typical pipe lift, spaced for even load
  • Counterweight: swing out for added stability with heavy pipe
  • Load chart: always know capacity at angle, radius, configuration
  • Tag lines: manage swing without hands on the load
  • Communication: radio + hand signals — one designated signaller
  • Working over the rear: reduces capacity significantly
Never lift over workers. Establish clear exclusion zones under and adjacent to suspended pipe.

Ground Disturbance

Pipeline work is heavily regulated for ground disturbance. Always:

  • Call before you dig — BC One Call / Alberta One-Call (free)
  • Wait the required notice period (typically 3 business days)
  • Use locate marks — color codes apply
  • Hand-dig within 1 m of marked utilities (the "tolerance zone")
  • Hydrovac for high-risk crossings
  • Document every locate, photo every cross

Confined Space

Trench at certain depths plus pump houses, valve boxes, vaults qualify as confined spaces.

  • Permit required before entry
  • Atmospheric testing: O2 (19.5–23.5%), LEL <10%, toxics under TLV
  • Continuous gas monitoring during work
  • Standby attendant outside, in constant communication
  • Rescue plan in place — NEVER enter to rescue without proper equipment (most confined space deaths are would-be rescuers)

Pipe Bedding & Backfill

  • Bedding: typically 100–150 mm clean sand or fine gravel
  • Pipe support: continuous bedding, no point loading
  • Initial backfill: hand-place to top of pipe, compact in lifts
  • Compaction: 95% Standard Proctor typical for non-roadway, 100% Modified under roads
  • Lift thickness: 200–300 mm loose, depending on equipment
  • Warning tape: placed 300 mm above pipe

Numbers to Remember

  • H2S OEL: 10 ppm (8 hr) · STEL 15 ppm · IDLH 100 ppm
  • Trench protection: 1.2 m+ in unstable soil
  • Type C soil slope: 1.5:1 (34°)
  • Spoil pile setback: 0.6 m (2 ft) min
  • Trench access: ladder every 7.5 m
  • Confined space O2: 19.5–23.5% safe range
  • LEL trigger: 10% of lower explosive limit
  • Hand-dig zone: within 1 m of marked utility
