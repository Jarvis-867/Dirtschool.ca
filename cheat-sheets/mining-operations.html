Haul road maintenance is critical. Potholes, washboard, and dust = increased wear, fuel consumption, accidents. Grader passes scheduled regularly.

Right of Way — Haul Road Rules

  • Loaded trucks always have right of way. Empty trucks pull over.
  • Larger machines have right of way over smaller ones.
  • Light vehicles yield to all heavy equipment.
  • At intersections: stop, radio, confirm clear before proceeding.
  • Following distance: 5 truck lengths minimum, more on grades.
  • Passing: only in designated areas, with radio confirmation.

Mining-Specific Safety

Bench Operation

  • Bench height set by equipment reach (excavator/shovel)
  • Berm at the crest — minimum 2/3 tire height of largest vehicle
  • Stand-off distance from crest: 1.5× bench height minimum
  • Daily inspection of bench face for cracks, undercuts, loose material

Blast Awareness

  • Know the daily blast schedule
  • Move equipment to designated blast park area before shot
  • All-clear signal required before re-entering blast zone
  • Watch for misfires and post-blast hazards (loose material, cracks)

Dust & Air Quality

  • Silica dust = serious lung hazard (silicosis, lung cancer)
  • Water trucks suppress dust on haul roads
  • Cab pressurization with HEPA filters in modern equipment
  • Respirator required outside cab in high-dust conditions

Truck Loading — Best Practice

  • Approach: truck backs into position, parks on level pad
  • Loader/shovel positioning: first bucket at front of box, even distribution
  • Pass count: 3-pass loading typical for matched fleet
  • Avoid overload: exceeds 110% of rated payload = wear, accidents
  • Avoid underload: increases cost per tonne
  • Never load over operator cab — swing path matters

Production Math

Cycle time: Load + Haul + Dump + Return + Wait
Production (tonnes/hr): (Payload × 60) ÷ Cycle time (min)
Cost per tonne: Total cost ÷ Total tonnes moved
Match factor: (Trucks × Loader cycle) ÷ (Truck cycle)

Match factor of 1.0 = trucks and loader are perfectly balanced. Less than 1.0 = loader sits idle. More than 1.0 = trucks queue.

Numbers to Remember

  • Berm height: 2/3 tire height of largest vehicle
  • Haul road grade: 8% against load, 10% favorable
  • Two-way road width: 3.5× widest vehicle
  • Following distance: 5 truck lengths min
  • Stand-off from bench crest: 1.5× bench height
  • Loaded trucks: always right of way
  • Match factor target: 1.0 (loader-truck balance)
