PPE Standard Requirements

  • Hard hat: ANSI Z89.1 — Type I (vertical impact), Class E for electrical
  • Safety glasses: ANSI Z87.1, side shields required
  • Hi-vis: Class 2 minimum for general construction, Class 3 for highway
  • Steel-toe boots: ASTM F2413, impact + compression rated
  • Hearing protection: Required at 85 dB TWA exposure
  • Gloves, respirators: Job-specific

Machine Operations — Key Rules

Excavator

  • Idle/warm up before operating
  • Spoil pile ≥2 ft from trench edge
  • Never swing over truck cab — load from side or rear
  • Slope limit ~30° (machine-dependent)
  • Lower bucket and engage swing brake before exiting

Dozer

  • Travel up/down slopes, not across
  • Most efficient push distance: under 300 ft
  • Slot dozing retains material between passes
  • Float blade for finish grade work

Wheel Loader

  • Carry bucket low during travel for stability
  • V-pattern (45° approach) most efficient for truck loading
  • Never load over operator cab
  • Articulated — turn with throttle, not just steering

Motor Grader

  • Moldboard angle 30–45° for general grading
  • Use leaning wheels for sharper cuts and stability
  • Articulated frame for tight turns and offset work
  • Fine grading is the primary purpose — not heavy excavation

Practical Eval — Auto-Fail Items

These will end your test instantly:
  • Operating without seatbelt
  • Ignoring or arguing with the spotter
  • Swinging over operator cab of haul truck
  • Operating with major defect
  • Unsafe shutdown (leaving bucket up, no parking brake)
  • Striking workers, equipment, or structures

Critical Numbers

  • Cubic feet in cubic yard: 27
  • OSHA trench protection: 5 ft+ depth
  • Spoil pile setback: 2 ft
  • Hearing protection threshold: 85 dB
  • Soil C max slope: 1.5:1
  • Most efficient dozer push: <300 ft
  • Loader approach: V-pattern, 45°
