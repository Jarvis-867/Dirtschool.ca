Trenches deeper than 1.2 m (4 ft) in BC, or as defined provincially, require a protective system: sloping, shoring, or shielding. Always check current WorkSafe regs.

Daily Inspection — Walk-Around

Outside the Machine

  • Tracks/tires — wear, tension, damage
  • Undercarriage — leaks, debris, damage
  • Hydraulic cylinders — leaks, damage
  • Pins, bushings, attachment points
  • Bucket/blade — teeth, edges, cracks
  • Fuel level, fluid levels
  • Lights, mirrors, glass
  • Belts, hoses, filters

In the Cab

  • Seatbelt, seat condition
  • Controls, joysticks — full range
  • Gauges and warning lights
  • Horn, backup alarm
  • Wipers, defroster, AC
  • Fire extinguisher, first aid
  • ROPS/FOPS intact, no damage
  • Logbook present, filled
Major defect found? Tag out. Don't operate. Report to supervisor immediately.

Excavator — Key Operating Rules

  • Travel on slopes: Boom uphill, bucket low. Idler forward going up, sprocket forward going down.
  • Swing path: Never over truck cab. Side or rear loading only.
  • Cab side digging: Better visibility. Avoid digging over the counterweight.
  • Track tension: Too tight = sprocket wear. Too loose = derailment.
  • Swing brake: Engages when on slopes or when leaving the seat.
  • Slew radius: Always check before swinging. Spotter near workers.

Dozer — Blade Types

BladeBest For
S (Straight)Heavy push, ripping, no tilt
U (Universal)Long-distance push, high capacity, curved blade holds material
SU (Semi-Universal)Compromise — push + some side casting
A (Angle)Side-casting, ditching, up to ~25° pivot
PAT (Power Angle Tilt)Full hydraulic angle and tilt from cab — fine grading

Dozing Techniques

  • Slot dozing: Push in a slot using spilled material as side walls
  • Side-by-side dozing: Two dozers blade-to-blade, more material per pass
  • Down-hill dozing: Use gravity, shorter cycles
  • Float blade: For finish grading, lets blade follow ground

Canadian Regs — High-Yield Facts

  • WHMIS 2015: GHS-aligned. Pictograms, signal words (Danger/Warning), Safety Data Sheets.
  • Fisheries Act §36: Prohibits depositing deleterious substances in fish-bearing waters.
  • OH&S Right to Refuse: Worker can refuse unsafe work without penalty.
  • OH&S Right to Know: Hazards must be communicated (training, SDS, signage).
  • OH&S Right to Participate: JOHS committees, safety meetings.
  • BC One Call / Alberta One-Call: Locate underground utilities BEFORE digging.
  • Riparian zones: Setbacks, sediment control, often permits required.

Numbers to Memorize

  • Pass mark: 70%
