Hierarchy of Hazard Controls

Apply controls in this order. Most effective at the top.

OrderControlExample
1EliminationRemove the hazard entirely (cancel the task, design out the danger)
2SubstitutionReplace with something less dangerous (different chemical, different method)
3EngineeringIsolate workers from hazard (guards, shoring, ventilation, barriers)
4AdministrativeProcedures, training, signage, work scheduling
5PPELast line of defense — gloves, hard hats, respirators
PPE is last on purpose. If you're relying only on PPE, you've missed steps 1–4.

FLHA Process — Field Level Hazard Assessment

Before every task, every shift. Documented.

  1. Identify the task — what specifically is being done?
  2. List the steps — break the task into 3–7 phases
  3. Identify hazards at each step — what could go wrong?
  4. Rank risk — likelihood × severity (low/med/high)
  5. Apply controls — using the hierarchy above
  6. Re-rank with controls in place — confirm risk is acceptable
  7. All workers sign — acknowledges they understand
  8. Stop work and update if conditions change

PPE Requirements — Standard Site

PPERequirement
Hard hatCSA Z94.1 / ANSI Z89.1, replace if cracked or struck
Hi-visClass 2 minimum, Class 3 for highway/night
Safety bootsCSA green triangle (Canada) / ASTM F2413, 6"+ ankle support
Eye protectionCSA Z94.3 / ANSI Z87.1, side shields, face shield for grinding
GlovesTask-specific — leather for general, cut-rated for steel, chemical for sour gas
Hearing protectionRequired at 85 dBA TWA, NRR rating matched to environment
RespiratorN95 minimum for nuisance dust, half-face for silica/asbestos with cartridges

Right to Refuse Unsafe Work

  1. Worker reports unsafe condition to supervisor
  2. Supervisor investigates with worker present
  3. If unresolved, JOHS committee or worker rep involved
  4. If still unresolved, regulator (WorkSafeBC, AB OH&S) called
  5. Worker stays at site, paid, doing other safe work until resolved
You can't be fired or punished for exercising the right to refuse, as long as you follow the proper process. Document everything.

Incident Response

Worker Injury

  1. Don't move them unless immediate danger
  2. Call 911 / first aid attendant
  3. Secure the scene
  4. Notify supervisor, document
  5. WorkSafe / WCB report within required time (varies by jurisdiction)

Equipment Damage / Near Miss

  1. Stop work in the area
  2. Take photos before moving anything
  3. Tag out damaged equipment
  4. Investigate root cause, not just symptoms
  5. Document and circulate lessons learned

Quick Reference

  • Hierarchy of control: Eliminate → Substitute → Engineer → Administer → PPE
  • Hearing protection trigger: 85 dBA
  • Hi-vis: Class 2 standard, Class 3 for highway
  • FLHA: every task, every shift, signed
  • Toolbox talk: daily, 5 min, documented
  • Right to refuse: stay on site, paid, follow process
