The E-C-L-M-A-B Method

Six-letter system for the walk-around. Same order every time means you don't miss anything.

CCabMirrors, glass, wipers, controls, gauges, seatbelt
LLightsAll running, signal, brake, marker, hazard, reverse
MMid-VehicleFrame, fuel tanks, exhaust, drivelines, batteries
AAxles & TiresTires, rims, lug nuts, hub seals, brakes, springs

Air Brake Tests — Sequence

Memorize this order. Examiners pay attention to the sequence, not just the result.

  1. Build air to governor cut-out (typically 120-135 psi). Should reach in under 2 minutes from 85→100 psi.
  2. Pump pedal to test cut-in. Compressor should kick back in around 100 psi.
  3. Static air loss test. Engine off, foot off brake. Watch gauge for 1 minute.
    • Single unit: max 3 psi/min loss
    • Combination: max 4 psi/min loss
  4. Applied air loss test. Engine off, full brake application held for 1 minute.
    • Single unit: max 4 psi/min loss
    • Combination: max 6 psi/min loss
  5. Low air warning. Pump pedal until warning activates — must trigger by 60 psi (or 55 in some provinces).
  6. Spring brake application. Continue pumping. Spring brakes (yellow knob) should pop out between 20-45 psi.
  7. Pushrod stroke check. With service brake fully applied, measure pushrod travel. Type 30 chamber: max ~1.5". Each chamber type has its own spec.
  8. Parking brake test. Yellow knob in (parking applied). Try to pull forward in low gear — truck should not move.

Major vs Minor Defects

This is what fails you on the test. Know which is which — examiners ask.

MAJOR (vehicle out of service): Brakes out of adjustment · Air loss exceeds limit · Steering play >30° · Tire below minimum tread (2/32" steer, 1/32" other) · Cracked rim · Loose lug nuts · Hub seal leaking onto brakes · Cracked frame · Inoperative low-air warning · Coupling defective
MINOR (repair within reasonable time): Single light burned out · Mud flap missing · Wiper streaking · Minor body damage · Small leak not affecting safety · Worn but legal tread

Coupling Inspection (Class 1)

  1. Inspect fifth wheel — grease, cracks, mounting bolts, slide rails locked
  2. Check kingpin — no cracks, no excessive wear, properly seated in jaws
  3. Verify locking jaws fully closed around kingpin (visual + tug test)
  4. Safety latch / locking handle in correct position
  5. Air lines — red (emergency) and blue (service) — no leaks, no cracks, secured
  6. Electrical pigtail connected, no fraying
  7. Trailer landing gear fully retracted, crank handle secured
  8. Tug test: low gear forward, gentle pull — confirms coupling solid

10-Second Number Cheat

  • Build time: 85→100 psi in < 2 min
  • Static loss: 3 psi/min single, 4 psi/min combo
  • Applied loss: 4 psi/min single, 6 psi/min combo
  • Low air warning: activates by 60 psi
  • Spring brakes apply: 20-45 psi
  • Cut-in / cut-out: ~100 / ~125 psi
  • Steer tread: 4/32" min · Other: 2/32" min
  • Steering play: max 30° wheel free play
