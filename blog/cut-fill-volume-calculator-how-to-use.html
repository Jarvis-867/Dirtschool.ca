Cut and Fill Volume Calculator — How to Use One

The Three Volume Units You Need to Know

Every cut/fill calculation involves three different states of material — and they all have different volumes:

  • BCY / BCM (Bank): material in the ground, undisturbed. This is what you measure on the site plan. Always start here.
  • LCY / LCM (Loose): material after it's been excavated and broken up. It swells — takes up more space. This is what goes in the truck.
  • CCY / CCM (Compacted): material after it's been placed and compacted. It shrinks. This is what you need for fills and embankments.

A cut/fill calculator works in bank volume. You apply swell and shrinkage factors to convert to the other units.

Basic Cut Volume Formula

Volume (BCY) = Length × Width × Depth ÷ 27

All measurements in feet. Divide by 27 to convert cubic feet to cubic yards.

Volume (BCM) = Length × Width × Depth

All measurements in metres. Result is directly in cubic metres.

📐 Example: Building Pad Cut

Pad area: 80 ft × 100 ft
Average cut depth: 2 ft
Volume = 80 × 100 × 2 ÷ 27 = 593 BCY

Average Depth Method

On real sites, the cut depth isn't uniform across the whole area. The average depth method takes spot elevations on a grid, calculates the cut or fill at each point, and averages them.

  1. Walk the site with a laser level and grade rod
  2. Take elevation shots on a 10–20 ft grid
  3. Record existing vs proposed elevation at each point
  4. Subtract existing from proposed: negative = cut, positive = fill
  5. Average the depths across all grid points
  6. Multiply average depth by total area

This is exactly what a cut/fill calculator automates. You enter the grid points and it calculates total cut volume, total fill volume, and whether the site balances.

Applying Swell Factor to Truck Loads

Once you have your BCY volume, you need to convert to LCY to figure out truck loads. This is where people get burned most often.

LCY = BCY × (1 + Swell Fraction)
📐 Example: 593 BCY of common earth (25% swell)

LCY = 593 × 1.25 = 741 LCY
Tandem truck carries 12 LCY
Loads = 741 ÷ 12 = 62 loads

Without swell: 593 ÷ 12 = 49 loads — you'd be 13 loads short and over budget.

Applying Shrinkage to Fill

If you're placing and compacting fill material, the compacted volume is what you need in the ground. You'll need more loose material than the compacted volume.

LCY to Import = CCY Required ÷ Compaction Factor (~0.85 for common earth)

Checking Site Balance

On a balanced site, the cut material becomes the fill material — you're not hauling anything off or importing anything. To check if your site balances:

Available Fill (CCY) = Cut Volume (BCY) × Shrinkage Factor

Compare this to the fill required. If available fill > fill required, you have excess to haul off. If less, you need to import.

📐 Example: Site Balance Check

Cut: 600 BCY of common earth | Shrinkage factor: 0.85
Available fill = 600 × 0.85 = 510 CCY
Fill required: 480 CCY
Result: 30 CCY surplus → haul off a few loads
