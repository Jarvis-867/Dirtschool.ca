Backhoe Comparison 2026: JCB vs Cat vs Kubota (Which One Pays for Itself?)

Published April 24, 2026

JCB 3CX (Mid-Size Backhoe)

  • Dig depth: 5.5m
  • Bucket capacity: 0.76–1.0 cy
  • Operating weight: 9.3t
  • Fuel consumption: ~7 L/hr (full duty)
  • Daily fuel cost (8 hrs): ~$56 (@ $1/L)
  • Loader breakout: 16 kN
  • Strengths: Fastest cycle times, most responsive controls, legendary parts availability
  • Weaknesses: Highest hourly rental (~$60–65/hr), slightly higher maintenance
  • Resale after 5 years: 55–60% of new price (best in class)

Cat 432F2 (Caterpillar Backhoe)

  • Dig depth: 5.3m
  • Bucket capacity: 0.70–0.92 cy
  • Operating weight: 8.8t
  • Fuel consumption: ~6.5 L/hr (full duty)
  • Daily fuel cost (8 hrs): ~$52 (most efficient)
  • Loader breakout: 15.5 kN
  • Strengths: Best fuel economy, lower operating cost, solid reliability
  • Weaknesses: Slightly less responsive hydraulics, fewer dealer shops than JCB
  • Resale after 5 years: 50–55% of new price

Kubota U35-4 (Compact Backhoe)

  • Dig depth: 4.8m (shallow)
  • Bucket capacity: 0.63 cy (small)
  • Operating weight: 7.5t (lighter)
  • Fuel consumption: ~5.5 L/hr (most efficient)
  • Daily fuel cost (8 hrs): ~$44 (cheapest to run)
  • Loader breakout: 13 kN
  • Strengths: Lowest cost, best for tight spaces, ideal for residential/landscaping
  • Weaknesses: Smallest bucket, shallow dig, low resale value, slower cycle
  • Resale after 5 years: 35–45% of new price

5-Year Cost Comparison (2,000 hrs/year)

Cost per hour $12.63 $11.38 $9.45

The Verdict

  • Doing aggressive earthwork? JCB wins (cycle speed = faster production = higher margins).
  • Running utility work? Cat or Kubota (fuel savings beat production losses).
  • Tight budget? Kubota (lowest operating cost, but resale is weak).
