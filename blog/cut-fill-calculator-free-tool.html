The Problem with Estimating Volumes

Most operators know the formula:

Volume = Length × Width × Depth

But that's rectangular cuts. Real job sites are messy:

  • Cuts with sloped sides (not vertical)
  • Irregular shapes
  • Fill areas with compaction math
  • Conversions between metric and imperial

Doing all this by hand takes time. Using a calculator app online means you need cell signal. On site? Good luck.

How the Free Cut & Fill Calculator Works

You enter:

  • Length (meters or feet)
  • Width (meters or feet)
  • Depth (meters or feet)
  • Metric or imperial

It instantly gives you:

  • Total cubic volume
  • Tonnage (based on soil type density)

No signup. No loading time. No internet required. Just fast, accurate math.

Real Example: Estimating a Driveway Grading Job

Job Specs:

Driveway: 50 meters long, 5 meters wide, 0.3 meters cut
You need to remove topsoil and dirt.

Using the calculator:

50 × 5 × 0.3 = 75 cubic meters of dirt to haul

At standard density (1.6 tonnes per cubic meter): 120 tonnes

Using 20-tonne haul trucks: 6 truck loads

Time to estimate: 10 seconds

Time doing math by hand: 10 minutes (if you're fast)

Why This Matters for Your Bottom Line

Fast, accurate volume calculations = competitive bids and less risk.
