Air Brake Test BC & Yukon — Questions, Answers & Numbers You Need to Know (2026)

April 2026  ·  7 min read  ·  Dirt School

The Q endorsement (Air Brake) is required on your licence before you can drive any vehicle equipped with air brakes. That means Class 1 and most Class 3 commercial vehicles.

The air brake knowledge test is separate from your main knowledge test. In BC, you write it at ICBC. Passing score is 80%. The questions are straightforward if you know the numbers — and there are specific numbers you absolutely have to memorize.

How the Air Brake System Works

Understanding the system makes the numbers make sense.

Compressor: Engine-driven. Pumps air into the reservoirs. Cuts out when pressure hits governor cut-out (~125–135 PSI). Cuts back in when pressure drops to governor cut-in (~100–105 PSI).

Reservoirs (tanks): Store compressed air. Usually at least two — service reservoir and supply reservoir. Drain them daily in cold weather to remove moisture.

S-cam brakes: The most common foundation brake type. When you press the brake pedal, air flows to the brake chambers. The pushrod pushes the slack adjuster, which rotates the S-cam shaft, which spreads the brake shoes against the drum. That's what slows you down.

Pushrod stroke: The distance the pushrod travels when brakes are applied. Stamped on the side of each brake chamber is the maximum allowable stroke. Exceed it = brake is out of adjustment = major defect.

Tractor protection valve: Sits between the tractor and trailer air lines. If the trailer supply line pressure drops to around 20–45 PSI (say the trailer breaks away), this valve closes automatically — keeping air in the tractor system so the tractor's brakes still work.

The Pre-Trip Air Brake Inspection — Step by Step

  1. Build pressure — run engine at fast idle until governor cuts out
  2. Turn off engine
  3. Fan the brakes — pump the brake pedal repeatedly to reduce pressure
  4. Low pressure warning must activate at or before 60 PSI
  5. Spring brakes apply at approximately 20–45 PSI
  6. Rebuild pressure — start engine, charge to operating pressure
  7. Static loss test (brakes off): engine off, note pressure, wait 1 minute — drop must be ≤ 2 PSI
  8. Static loss test (brakes on): hold foot brake, note pressure, wait 1 minute — drop must be ≤ 4 PSI
  9. Check pushrod stroke — apply brakes and observe each chamber

Practice Questions — Air Brakes

Q: At what pressure must the low pressure warning activate?
At or before 60 PSI (552 kPa). It must activate before pressure drops below 60 PSI — not at exactly 60.
Q: With the engine off and brakes released, how much pressure loss per minute is acceptable?
No more than 2 PSI (14 kPa) per minute for a single vehicle.
Q: What is the purpose of the tractor protection valve?
To protect the tractor's air supply if the trailer air line is severed or the trailer breaks away. When trailer supply pressure drops to ~20–45 PSI, the valve closes automatically.
Q: Pushrod stroke exceeds the stamped limit on the brake chamber. What does this mean?
Major defect — the brake is out of adjustment and the vehicle cannot be driven until repaired.
Q: What is the S-cam's function?
When rotated by the slack adjuster, the S-cam spreads the brake shoes outward against the drum, creating braking force.
Q: You apply the service brakes at 100 PSI and check pressure after 1 minute. It has dropped 8 PSI. What do you do?
Do not drive. The loss rate (8 PSI/min with brakes applied) exceeds the allowable 4 PSI/min limit — major defect.
Q: At what pressure do spring brakes apply automatically?
Approximately 20–45 PSI, depending on the system. This is the emergency brake application as air pressure drops.
Q: You build air pressure with the engine at fast idle. After 3 minutes, pressure is only at 65 PSI. Is this a concern?
Yes. A healthy system should reach 100 PSI within approximately 3 minutes at fast idle. Slow build-up may indicate a compressor problem or significant air leak.

What the Knowledge Test Looks Like

The BC air brake knowledge test is multiple choice — typically 30 questions. You need 80% to pass. The questions cover:

  • Component names and functions (compressor, reservoirs, S-cam, slack adjusters, chambers)
  • Pressure limits (60 PSI warning, 100–130 PSI operating, loss rates)
  • Pre-trip inspection procedure
  • Major defect identification
  • Tractor protection valve operation
  • Emergency brake procedures

The test isn't tricky. Know the numbers, understand what each component does, and you'll pass it without drama.
