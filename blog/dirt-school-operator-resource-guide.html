Dirt School: A Complete Look at the Operator-Built Resource Helping People Break Into Heavy Equipment

Published Apr 26, 2026 | 12 min read

If you're trying to break into the heavy equipment trade — whether that's running iron for the IUOE in BC, chasing your Red Seal in Alberta, or testing for NCCER in the States — the biggest hurdle isn't the work. It's the test. And the gap between knowing how to operate a machine and being able to put that knowledge on paper has wrecked more aspiring operators than any boss ever has.

That's the gap Dirt School was built to close.

This is a thorough walkthrough of three connected resources: dirtschool.ca (the study and content hub), dirtschool.gumroad.com (where the guides live), and dirtcalculator.ca (the completely free web app full of operator math tools). Together they form one of the only end-to-end study and field resources written by an actual operator, for actual operators.

Why This Exists in the First Place

Most aptitude prep books were written by textbook publishers. They use train problems, pure geometry, and word problems about pipes filling tanks. None of it looks like the work. None of it sounds like the work. And if you've been on iron for ten years and haven't seen a written test since high school, that disconnect is brutal.

The person behind Dirt School grew up in the heavy equipment industry in BC and the Yukon, working camp jobs every summer on highway projects. No formal tickets at first — just learning from old-timers on real job sites. That perspective is the whole reason these guides feel different. The math examples use Cat 330 bucket capacities and haul truck loads, not trains. The mechanical reasoning sections use the gears, levers, and pulleys you actually see on a machine. The reading comprehension passages sound like safety regs and dispatch notes, because that's what shows up on the test.

The stated mission is blunt: you haven't been in a classroom in ten years, you know how to run iron, what you need is someone to translate the test — not explain the trade from scratch.

The Guides — What's on Gumroad

When you're ready to go deeper, the paid guides live at dirtschool.gumroad.com. They're $9.99 each (one is $9), instant PDF downloads, no subscription.

Test Prep Guides

IUOE Aptitude Test — BC & Yukon (Local 115)

The flagship. Nineteen chapters covering math, mechanical reasoning, reading, and spatial. A 40-question practice test with answer key. A 7-day study plan and a 10-minute cram sheet for the day of. A breakdown of how the test tries to trick you — unit traps, "which is NOT" questions, conservative-answer patterns. 139 pages.

IUOE Aptitude Test — Local 955 Alberta Edition

Built specifically for operators applying in Edmonton, Calgary, Fort McMurray, and Edson. Alberta-specific content throughout — oil sands, pipeline, H2S safety. Eight diagrams covering gears, levers, pulleys, and rotation. A 60-question full mock test. Real 2024 wage data: $44–$82/hr by classification.

Red Seal HEO — Complete Study Guide

For operators working toward Red Seal endorsement. Covers both Dozer and Excavator streams. Based on the National Occupational Analysis. 100-question practice test. Canadian regulations including WorkSafe, WHMIS, and Fisheries Act compliance. Field-vs-exam callouts throughout so you know when "the right answer on the test" differs from "what you'd actually do."

NCCER Heavy Equipment Operations — USA

Levels 1–3 curriculum, written and practical evaluation prep. OSHA standards, all machine types (excavator, dozer, grader, loader). 40-question practice test. The section on the practical eval — what gets you failed instantly — is the kind of thing you only learn from people who've sat through it.

Licensing Guides

Class 1 / Class 3 Road Test — BC & Yukon

Pre-trip plus air brakes plus the Q endorsement. The full E-C-L-M-A-B pre-trip sequence. Air brake static loss test procedure. Major-vs-minor defect criteria — what's a fail, what's a comment. 50-question mock test plus a printable one-page cheat sheet.

Class 1 / Class 3 Road Test — Alberta

Same depth, Alberta-specific. Pushrod stroke, road test scoring, backing technique, coupling for Class 1. Three printable pre-trip checklists and practice score sheets.

Field & Career Guides

Estimating for Small Contractors

If you're going out on your own, this is the bid math nobody teaches you. Cut/fill, swell and shrinkage, truck loads, equipment cost and production rates, markup vs margin (they're not the same thing). Six pre-built bid templates for common job types — grading, driveway, demo, etc. Fillable estimate sheets and a job tracking log.

Job Site Safety & Toolbox Talks

A 58-page workbook for operators, foremen, and small contractors. Ten FLHA forms with hazard ID, PPE checklists, and crew sign-off. Twenty pre-written toolbox talks ready to run on a Monday morning. JHA forms, pre-job safety meeting templates. WHMIS 2015, OH&S rights, first aid quick reference. Emergency contact sheets you can print for every site.

Heavy Equipment Operator Log Book ($9)

Ninety days of daily logs — hours, production, delays, signatures. Pre-shift inspection checklist (22 items). Fuel and hour meter logs. Maintenance records. Incident and near-miss reports. Reference charts for grade stakes, slopes, and conversions.

Owner-Operator Startup Guide

Everything for going independent. Provincial business registration, insurance types and cost ranges, equipment financing (lease vs buy), finding and bidding your first jobs, crew hiring and payroll, year-one cash flow planning.

Mining Operations — Equipment & Safety

For mining, aggregates, and quarry operators. Open-pit and underground pit layout, mobile crushing and screening, haul road construction, mining-specific PPE and regs, production tracking and cost-per-tonne.

First Year Apprentice — Survival Guide

This one's the antidote to the YouTube version of the trade. Camp life, bunkhouses, learning iron in the field vs in class, working with experienced operators without looking stupid, the common first-year mistakes, and the mental health and money management piece nobody talks about.

Pipeline Operator — Trenching & Installation

Trench design under OSHA and WorkSafe standards. Side boom operation. Soil types and slope angles. Backfill and compaction specs. Confined space hazards. Working with survey and pipeline crews.

That's twelve paid guides covering the complete arc from "I've never written a test" through "I'm running my own outfit."

Why This Approach Actually Works for People Getting In

The standard advice for someone trying to enter the trade is some combination of "apply to the union and wait," "go to a private school for $15,000," or "find someone to apprentice you." Those paths still exist and still work for some people. But there's a fourth path that most resources skip: get yourself test-ready and field-literate before you walk into the first interview.

Here's why Dirt School works for that path:

It Treats You Like an Adult

The whole tone assumes you're capable, you're motivated, and you've just been out of school for a while. It doesn't explain what a wrench is. It explains why the test asks about levers in a particular way and how to recognize the trap.

The Free Tier Is Genuinely Useful, Not Bait

The practice questions on the blog, the cram sheet, the calculators — those alone are more than most paid courses give you. If you used nothing but the free resources and worked through them seriously, you'd walk into the IUOE aptitude test better prepared than the average applicant.

The Paid Guides Are Priced Like Reference Books

Twenty-nine dollars per guide is what a paperback costs at the airport. Compare that to a $400 prep course or a $15,000 private school and the value math is obvious. Buy the one that matches your test, study it for a week, and you're done.

The Progression Is Built In

Start with the test guide for your region. Pass the test, get on with a contractor. Pick up the Log Book to track your hours properly. A few years in, when you're thinking about going on your own, the Estimating guide and Owner-Operator Startup Guide are waiting. The whole arc of a career is mapped — you're not buying a one-time test prep, you're buying into a resource that grows with you.

It's Regional Where It Matters

Separate guides for IUOE Local 115 (BC/Yukon) and Local 955 (Alberta). Separate Class 1/3 road test guides for BC and Alberta because the inspection sequences and scoring are different. NCCER is built around US standards. That regional precision is the difference between a guide that helps and a guide that wastes your time.
