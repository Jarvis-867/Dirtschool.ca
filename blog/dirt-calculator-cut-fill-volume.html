Dirt Calculator: Cut & Fill Volume Calculator for Jobsite Math

Posted April 16, 2026 | Tools | Read time: 5 min

One of the most common jobsite questions: "How much dirt did we move today?"

The answer used to require a notepad, a calculator you can't use when you're muddy, and mental math. Now? A simple dirt calculator gives you the answer in seconds.

What is a Dirt Calculator?

A dirt calculator computes the volume of earth moved or needed for excavation, grading, or filling work. Instead of manual formulas, you plug in dimensions and get instant results in metric or imperial.

Why Operators Need a Dirt Calculator

  • Jobsite speed: Answer "how much did we move" instantly, no guessing
  • Accuracy: Eliminates mental math errors that cost contracts
  • Offline: Works without internet (critical on remote sites)
  • Metric + Imperial: Switch between cubic meters and cubic yards instantly
  • Payroll: Track volume for per-yard contracts or equipment billing

Common Dirt Calculator Formulas

Rectangular Cut/Fill (most common)

Volume = Length × Width × Depth

  • Example: 100m long × 20m wide × 1.5m deep = 3,000 m³
  • Convert to cubic yards: 3,000 ÷ 0.764 = 3,927 yd³

Trench Volume

Volume = Length × Width × Depth (same formula, narrower width)

  • Example: 500m trench × 2m wide × 1m deep = 1,000 m³

Sloped Cut (grading, banks)

Volume = Length × Average Depth × Width

  • If depth varies from 0m to 2m: use average (1m)
  • More complex slopes need survey data or GPS

Dirt Calculator Features to Look For

  • Metric & Imperial toggle: m³, yd³, cubic feet, tons
  • Multiple calculation types: Rectangular, trench, slope, circular
  • Offline access: Works without internet (app or cached web)
  • History log: Track calculations throughout the day
  • Export option: Save or email results to foreman
  • Mobile-friendly: Use on tablet or phone on jobsite

Real-World Example: Using a Dirt Calculator

Scenario: Grading a parking lot
  • Site size: 200m × 150m
  • Grade depth: average 0.8m (varies 0.5m to 1.2m)
  • Manual calculation: 200 × 150 × 0.8 = 24,000 m³
  • Convert to yards: 24,000 ÷ 0.764 = 31,412 yd³
  • With calculator: Enter dimensions → get answer in 5 seconds
  • Billing: Contractor pays per cubic yard. At $3/yd³ = ~$94,236

Dirt Calculator Mistakes to Avoid

  • Wrong units: Mixing metric and imperial (always convert first)
  • Not accounting for slope: Flat site math doesn't work on hillsides
  • Forgetting swell factor: Excavated dirt expands ~25% (loose vs compacted)
  • Using average depth on irregular sites: Survey tricky areas properly
  • Assuming consistency: Dirt type affects density and volume change
