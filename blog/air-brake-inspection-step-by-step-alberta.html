Air Brake Inspection Step by Step — Alberta Road Test

April 2026  ·  8 min read  ·  Dirt School

The air brake inspection is performed as the "A" step in your E-C-L-M-A-B pre-trip. For the Alberta Class 1 or Class 3 road test, the examiner will watch you work through the full sequence. Done right it takes 5–8 minutes. Done wrong, it ends your test early.

This is the exact sequence — in order.

The 8-Step Air Brake Inspection

1 Build Pressure to Governor Cut-Out

Start the engine. Wait for air pressure to build to governor cut-out — normally 120–125 PSI. This confirms the compressor is working and the governor is set correctly. Should take no more than 3 minutes from a cold start.

2 Test Governor Cut-In

Fan the brakes (pump the brake pedal quickly) to drop the pressure. The compressor should kick back in — governor cut-in — at approximately 100 PSI. If it cuts in below 85 PSI, that's a compressor fault. Note it.

3 Test the Low Pressure Warning

Continue fanning the brakes. As pressure drops, watch for the low pressure warning light and/or buzzer to activate. It must come on at 60 PSI or higher.

Major defect: Low pressure warning activates below 60 PSI, or doesn't activate at all. This is an automatic fail on your road test — the examiner checks this every time.

4 Static Pressure Loss Test — Brakes Released

Shut off the engine. Let pressure build back to full (120+ PSI) first. With the parking brake OFF and service brakes NOT applied:

  1. Note the starting pressure
  2. Wait exactly 1 minute
  3. Note the ending pressure
  4. Calculate loss: must be ≤2 PSI/min (single) or ≤3 PSI/min (combination)

Any more than that indicates an air leak — major defect.

5 Static Pressure Loss Test — Brakes Applied

Now apply and hold the service brake (foot on brake pedal, steady pressure):

  1. Note the starting pressure
  2. Hold the brake firmly for 1 minute
  3. Calculate loss: must be ≤3 PSI/min (single) or ≤4 PSI/min (combination)

Release the brake. If you heard or felt anything unusual — note it and check further.

6 Test the Parking Brake

Apply the parking brake (pull the yellow knob). On a slight grade if available, attempt to move forward gently. The vehicle should not move. This confirms the spring brakes are functioning. Release the parking brake.

7 Test the Service Brakes

At slow speed (5–10 km/h), apply the brakes firmly. The vehicle should stop straight with no pull to either side, no grab, no shimmy. Any pull or vibration indicates a brake fault — note it.

8 Visual Leak Check

With the engine running and pressure built up, walk the vehicle and listen for air leaks at fittings, hoses, brake chambers, and glad hand connections. An audible leak at 1 metre distance is a major defect.

Automatic fail: Audible air leak at normal conversation distance (1 metre). Don't ignore hissing sounds during your walk-around.

Pushrod Stroke — What to Check

With the brakes applied, the exposed pushrod on each brake chamber should not exceed the adjustment limit. The limit depends on the chamber type:

  • Type 30 chamber: maximum 2" (51mm) stroke
  • Type 24 chamber: maximum 2" (51mm) stroke
  • Long stroke (LS) chambers: maximum 2½" to 3" depending on size

Excess stroke means the brakes are out of adjustment — major defect on steer axles.

Glad Hands — Which Is Which

  • Blue glad hand (Emergency/Supply): charges the trailer air reservoir
  • Red glad hand (Service): controls the trailer service brakes

Always connect supply (blue) first, disconnect service (red) first. The examiner may ask you which is which — know it cold.

Q Endorsement Written Test

Before you can take your Class 1 or Class 3 road test with an air brake vehicle, you need the Q endorsement — which requires passing a separate written knowledge test at the registry. Study the components, the inspection sequence, and the key pressure numbers. The written test is multiple choice and focuses on these exact numbers and procedures.
