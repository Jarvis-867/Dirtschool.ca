WorkSafe: Regulations & Compliance

What is WorkSafe (Alberta)?

WorkSafeBC (BC), WorkSafeAB (Alberta), etc. are provincial agencies that enforce health & safety regulations. Employers and operators must comply.

Key Regulations (Alberta)

  • Trenching (>4ft): Shoring required. Competent person supervision.
  • Tip-over protection: ROPS (roll-over protective structure) on all dozers/excavators.
  • Fall protection: Hard hats, safety vests for all workers.
  • Utilities: Dig-Safe locates mandatory before ANY digging.
  • Equipment inspection: Daily pre-trips for all heavy equipment.
  • Operator qualifications: Some machines require certification (pipelayers, cranes).

Incident Reporting

Required if: Injury (any), property damage >$2,500, near-miss with high potential.

Timeline: Report within 24 hours to WorkSafe. Employer must investigate.

Do NOT blame operator. Focus on system failures (equipment, procedure, training).

Operator Responsibilities

  • Know safety rules for your equipment
  • Report hazards/unsafe conditions
  • Use required PPE (hard hat, safety vest)
  • Participate in JSA/FHIA meetings
  • Report incidents/near-misses promptly
  • Refuse unsafe work (you have the right)
