Backhoes: Compact Digging & Trenching

What is a Backhoe?

A backhoe is a small excavator with a front loader bucket and rear digging bucket. Versatile machine: digging, trenching, loading, material handling in tight spaces.

Key Specs

  • Digging depth: 4-6m typical (varies by model)
  • Bucket sizes: 0.4-0.8 cy (small, precise)
  • Weight: 8-12 tons (portable, fits on standard truck)
  • Footprint: Compact, works in confined areas (tight utility work)

Trenching Operations

Backhoes excel at utility trenching: water, gas, electrical lines. Digs to depth, material piles beside trench, then front loader cleans up or loads trucks.

Safety on Backhoes

  • Trench shoring: Deep trenches require shoring (safety requirement). Backhoe operator must know shoring requirements.
  • Utilities: Call Dig-Safe before digging. Backhoe can hit buried lines easily.
  • Stability: Don't overextend rear digging arm (tip-over risk).
