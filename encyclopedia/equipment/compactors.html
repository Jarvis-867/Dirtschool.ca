Compactors: Soil Compaction Equipment

Types of Compactors

TypeBest ForHow It Works
Vibratory RollerMost soil typesHeavy drum vibrates at high frequency (1,500-3,000 vpm). Best for sand, gravel, clay.
Smooth Wheel RollerAsphalt finishingHeavy steel drum rolls over material. Compacts and smooths.
Pneumatic RollerAsphalt, fine materialRubber tires, adjustable pressure. Gentle compaction for sensitive materials.
Plate CompactorTight spacesSmall vibrating plate. Ideal for utility work, small areas.

Compaction Process

  1. Spread material in 6-12" lifts (layers)
  2. Operate compactor in overlapping passes (2-4 passes typical)
  3. Test density (nuclear or sand cone test) to verify target reached
  4. Move to next lift

Safety on Compactors

  • Vibration: Operators experience high vibration. Take breaks to prevent health issues.
  • Noise: Wear hearing protection. Compactors are loud (85-95 dB).
  • Temperature: Drum gets hot. Don't touch, wear protective gear.
  • Visibility: Limited sight around machine. Use spotters.
